Nablus

Nablus is a historic city located in the northern West Bank,
known for its rich cultural heritage and vibrant atmosphere.
It has been a significant center of trade and culture since ancient times,
with a history dating back thousands of years. Nablus is famous for its bustling old city,
soap-making tradition, and delicious sweets, particularly *kanafeh*.
The city is also home to several historical landmarks, including Ottoman-era buildings,
ancient mosques, and the ruins of Sebastia nearby.
Despite modern challenges, Nablus remains a symbol of resilience and cultural pride.
